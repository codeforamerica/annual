Building Community as Capacity

In times of shrinking budgets and higher demands on government, it is essential to find new ways for local government to operate. Citizens are showing they can take on more responsibility and governments are seeing the community as capacity to help with things such as digging out fire hydrants in the snow, helping shape city plans and designs, and even rewriting government websites.

The Brigade provides a national platform to help these groups come together, and for others, the tools and resources to start a local chapter in their community. This growing network of civic leaders is working to redefine the notion of citizenship at the local level..

Meet a Local Leader

Christopher Whitaker

Brigade Captain, City of Chicago

Christopher co-hosts a weekly Open Gov Chicago Hack Night with other civic hacking leaders of the Open Gov Chicago-land community. When not at Hack Night or reporting on the state of civic hacking in Chicago, Christopher works at Smart Chicago Collaborative on projects like the Chicago User Testing (CUT) Group to ensure that civic tech is verified and validated by "regular Chicago residents who get paid to test out civic apps."

Jeffrey Johnson

Brigade Captain, City of San Diego

Jeffrey Johnson has repeatedly stood before his city council and advocated for civic tech innovation, open government and Code for America. He attends meetings with local city and regional government and hosts Brigade events all while holding down a full time job at Open Geo, where he is key personnel, and raising a young family.
