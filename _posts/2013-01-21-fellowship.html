Fellowship

The Fellowship connects developers, designers and urbanists with local governments for a year-long collaboration. Over the course of the program, Fellows and government partners build apps, explore new approaches to old problems, and tackle some of our cities' toughest challenges.

Andrew Hyder

2013 Fellow on Team Kansas City

"The Code for America Fellowship was as transformative for us as it was for Kansas City. When our year was done, those cities had changed us. Sure, they now have Innovation Departments, Open Data policies, and civic hacking volunteers. Yet we Fellows learned how to take the work that we know, our design, coding, and organizing skills, and apply it to something we love, improving our cities. I went from being some guy that made maps, to being a leader in the civic technology movement. Win win. And now I'm BBQ connoisseur too!"


Beth Niblock

CIO of Louisville, on the Criminal Justice Dashboard

"The Criminal Justice Commission has been waiting since about 1980 to have something like this. When they saw the first real iteration of the dashboard there were literally tears in peoples eyes because they could answer questions in real time and they had confidence in the data. It was just breathtaking."
