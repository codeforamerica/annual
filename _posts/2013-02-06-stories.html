2013 was a big and busy year.

We tackled harder problems like criminal justice in Louisville and food access in San Francisco. We grew and refined our programs — and then took them international. We began to look at the systematic policy problems around data and procurement. And we began to define how together we can transform our communities — in policy and practice. Hundreds of passionate citizens, many of whom are extremely talented technologists, joined our cause. Now more than ever, people around the world are coding for their communities.
