In 2013, the frustration surrounding the launch of healthcare.gov made it more clear than ever that the digital interfaces to government are critical. There’s a pressing need for citizens to lead the way, bringing new talent to solve our shared problems.

This year, we saw those leaders step up. Twenty-seven Fellows, hundreds of partners in local government, thousands of active volunteers, new international partners, and now more than a dozen civic startups — together we found new ways to transform our communities and refine our shared vision of what government by the people, for the people means in the 21st century.

The people in our network began to explore what it means when interfaces to government are decidedly not simple, beautiful, or easy to use: when they stand between you and feeding your family, your healthcare, or your child’s education. Fellows working in Louisville and New York City delved into the criminal justice system. In the Bay Area, teams tackled access to food assistance. And across the country, the CfA community addressed real problems facing our cities in the 21st century.

These experiences remind us what’s at stake in our work — and what's possible. We begin 2014 with a deepened commitment to work on what matters.

Thank you for a phenomenal year. We're humbled by the talent and passion of the Code for America community, and we hope you enjoy these reflections.

The Code for America Team
