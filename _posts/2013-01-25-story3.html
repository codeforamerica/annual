Helping Make Public Health Data Public

foodinspectiondata.us
‹ ›


Everyday governments gather massive amounts of data, about everything from building safety to restaurant cleanliness. This data is valuable not only to the City and its restaurant inspectors, but to the diners that patronize them. However, data is only useful -- and truly valuable -- when it's made available to the public. In 2013 Code for America made restaurant inspection readily available to diners -- by putting it on Yelp. Code for America collaborated with staff from the City of San Francisco and Yelp to create a standardized format for restaurant inspection data. Now any city that releases their restaurant inspection data in this standardized format can easily integrate it into yelp, and have it displayed on restaurant profile pages. Currently, four cities are on LIVES: San Francisco, x, x, and x. These cities are not only giving residents the information they need to keep themselves and their families healthy -- but hopefully inspiring restaurants everywhere to keep their kitchen's clean.
