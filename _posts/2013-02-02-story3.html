Putting Data in People's Hands

foodinspectiondata.us
‹ ›


Everyday governments gather massive amounts of data, about everything from building safety to restaurant cleanliness.

In 2013 Code for America made restaurant inspection data readily available to diners — by putting it on Yelp. We work with both goverment staff and the Yelp team to create a standardized format for this data, called LIVES.

Now any city that releases their restaurant inspections in this format can see it on Yelp's restaurant profile pages. Currently, four cities have done so: Louisville, San Francisco, Wake County, and x. These cities are giving residents information to keep their families healthy — and hopefully encouraging restaurants everywhere to keep their kitchens clean.

It's making public data useful by showing it to citizens at the point of decision-making — Where should I go for pad thai? — rather than buried deep in a city website.







"It's exciting to be a part of an important initiative to disseminate valuable health department information to the 84 million unique visitors that turn to Yelp each month on average.
Jeremy Stoppelman, CEO and Co-founder of Yelp
