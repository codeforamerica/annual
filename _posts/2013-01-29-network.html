2011

In 2011 we launched our first program, the Fellowship, and collaborated with three partner cities.

 2012

In 2012 we partnered with eight cities for the Fellowship, launched the Brigade in 16 communities, and began a pilot of the Peer Network.

 2013

In 2013 our network grew steadily, with Fellowship teams collaborating with 10 cities and counties, the Brigade spreading to 31 communities, and staff from 50+ cities joined the Peer Network.

 Innovation Offices

By 2013 more than 13 cities around the country had created offices or high-level roles focused on innovation (i.e. Office of New Urban Mechanics, Chief Innovation Officer). These serve as spaces for experimentation, building new skill sets, opening data, engaging the community, and demonstrating new ways of leveraging technology that can serve citizens better and save money.

 National Events

The network participated in two major events: Code Across America and National Day of Civic Hacking. During these coordinated events, citizens and governments convened at dozens of events to build apps, liberate datasets, and hold write-a-thons. Nine Brigade members were then honored with an invitation to a White House Champions of Change celebrating National Day of Civic Hacking.
