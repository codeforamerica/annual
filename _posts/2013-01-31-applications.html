WE ARE TECHNOLOGISTS

Talented technologists join the Code for America network to roll up their sleeves and work on civic tech. Whether it's building a new tool, redeploying an application in another city, scaling a project to serve more users, or standing up an open data portal, you earn your CfA track jacket by pushing code.

BUILDING NEW SOLUTIONS

In 2013 we tackled problems with technology — from giving South Bend residents a say about what happens to properties in the their neighborhood to giving judges in Lousiville real insight into the justice system. The apps have had a range of impacts, but one thing is consistent: all are open source and all are making society better.




SCALING APPS AND IMPACT

Fellowship apps were taken to new levels: version 2.0 of DiscoverBPS (built in 2011) is changing the way Bostonians select public schools, and Detroiters used TextMyBus (2012) to look up more than a million bus arrival times. When Austin saw the worst flood season in 50 years, people checked ATXfloods (2012) to stay safe.

Brigades were a powerful force for redeployment, with several apps "going viral" as volunteers around the country brought them local. Likewise, civic startups took their products to new cities nationwide.
