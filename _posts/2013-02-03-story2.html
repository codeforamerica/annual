Improving Government Transparency

recordtrac.com
‹ ›


The City of Oakland receives a huge volume of public records requests, but the existing system didn't respond to them efficiently, creating duplicative work and making it difficult to track outstanding inquiries — and it was confusing for users. As the City's employees strove to respond promptly, but they struggled with the quantity of requests, the internal process, and the tool that they used to fulfill them. As a result, tensions arose between residents and the City over request compliance.


The 2013 Fellows interviewed employees in every City department, from the Fire Department to IT, as well as residents and community groups, before building RecordTrac to streamline this records request process. It offers three key services: a searchable archive of past requests and responses, a simple form to make a new request, and a tool to track the status of a request. Over 2,000 requests had been made through the new tool by year's end.


By bringing transparency to the system, saving city staff time and improving responses, RecordTrac is a step to repair the relationship between residents and City Hall around transparency and public information.
